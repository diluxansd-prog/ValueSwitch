## #1 Talkmobile 50GB — best raw data per £

**The deal**: £8/mo for 50GB of 5G data, unlimited calls and texts, on a 12-month contract.

**What you get**: Genuinely 50GB on Vodafone's 5G network — same masts as Vodafone customers (see our [Vodafone vs Talkmobile guide](/guides/mobile/vodafone-vs-talkmobile-2026) for the trade-offs). At £8, this is the cheapest UK SIM-only deal we've seen with 50GB+ data.

**The catches**:
- 12-month contract, not rolling — early exit fee applies
- £1.99/day EU roaming (was 99p in 2024)
- No data rollover; unused GB expires monthly
- Customer service is chat-only during business hours

**Best for**: Heavy mobile data users who don't travel to Europe much and don't need rolling flexibility. £96/year is the cheapest you'll find for 50GB.

## #2 Lebara 30GB — best for travellers

**The deal**: £9/mo for 30GB of 4G/5G data, unlimited UK calls/texts, **free roaming in 39 countries**, 30-day rolling contract.

**What you get**: 30GB is enough for most people (UK average is 22GB/mo). The killer feature is genuinely free EU + Turkey + India + Pakistan + USA roaming — almost nobody else offers this.

**The catches**:
- Lebara uses Vodafone's network — same coverage as Talkmobile but fewer 5G areas
- Calls to non-Lebara numbers count against your monthly minutes (which are technically "unlimited" but capped at 5,000)
- The £9 price is intro-rate; goes to £11 after 6 months on some promo codes (read the small print)

**Best for**: Anyone who travels internationally even a couple of times a year. Saves £20-£40 per trip vs networks that charge daily roaming.

## #3 Smarty 100GB — best for power users

**The deal**: £10/mo for 100GB of 4G/5G data on Three's network, unlimited UK calls/texts, 30-day rolling contract.

**What you get**: 100GB is genuinely a lot — enough for daily Netflix in HD if you forget to use Wi-Fi. Smarty is owned by Three and uses Three's network, which has improved 5G coverage massively in 2025-2026.

**The catches**:
- £2/day EU roaming (capped at 12GB/day)
- Three's network was historically weaker in rural areas — check the Three coverage map for your address
- Smarty doesn't sell phones; SIM-only only
- Customer service is app + chat; no phone line

**Best for**: People who want a generous data allowance with the flexibility to cancel any month. The 100GB is the giveaway here — £10 is a ridiculous price for that much.

## #4 giffgaff 25GB — best for occasional roamers

**The deal**: £10/mo for 25GB of 4G/5G on O2's network, unlimited UK calls/texts, 30-day rolling, **free EU roaming up to 5GB**.

**What you get**: O2's network is the strongest in many UK rural areas (especially Scotland and Wales). giffgaff has unique features — data rollover, refer-a-friend bonuses, "WiFi calling" included.

**The catches**:
- 25GB isn't huge — you'll burn through it fast if you stream daily on cellular
- giffgaff has had 2 major outages in 2025 (more than O2 itself)
- After your 5GB EU cap, roaming gets capped to slow speeds (not extra-charged, just slowed)
- Customer service is community forum + chat; very few real humans

**Best for**: People in rural UK areas where O2 has the best signal, and who roam to Europe occasionally but don't need unlimited roaming.

## #5 1pMobile 30GB — best for fixed-price stability

**The deal**: £10/mo for 30GB on EE's network, **5GB EU roaming included**, 12-month contract with **guaranteed no price rise**.

**What you get**: EE has the best 5G network in the UK by most independent tests. 1pMobile inherits all of that. Their selling point is **fixed pricing for the full contract** — they're one of the few UK networks that doesn't bump prices in April.

**The catches**:
- 12-month contract — early exit fee applies
- 5GB EU roaming is a soft cap, not unlimited
- Their app is genuinely terrible (rated 2.1/5 in May 2026)
- Customer service is email only (no chat, no phone)

**Best for**: People who want the best UK network coverage at a budget price and value predictable billing over flexibility.

## The £10 trap to watch for

Several networks (we won't name names but they all advertise on London Tube) sell "£10 SIM" deals that are actually **£10 for 6 months, then £18-£25 thereafter**. The contract is 24 months. Net cost over 2 years: £350-£500.

To spot these:
- Look for "promo rate" or "intro rate" or "for the first 6 months" in the small print
- Check the **total contract cost** — should be £240 max for a £10/mo 24-month deal
- Avoid any contract where the "regular price" isn't clearly shown

## How much data do you actually need?

Most people overestimate. UK averages from Ofcom (2025):

- **Light user (mostly Wi-Fi)**: 5-10 GB/mo — £6-£8 plans cover it
- **Average user**: 22 GB/mo — £8-£12 plans cover it
- **Heavy user (cellular streaming)**: 40+ GB/mo — £10-£15 plans cover it
- **Super-heavy / hotspot**: 80+ GB/mo — £12-£18 plans cover it

Most people on £30+/mo "unlimited" plans use under 25GB. They're paying ~£10/mo extra for data they don't use.
