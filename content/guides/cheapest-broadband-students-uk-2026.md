You're moving into uni halls or a shared house in September. The landlord doesn't include broadband. You sign a 12-month contract — and then leave in June. You've just paid for 3 months of broadband at an empty house, plus a £35 early-termination fee.

This is the trap. It catches roughly **half a million UK students every academic year**, and most don't realise until they try to cancel. The good news: there's a small but growing market of **9-month student broadband contracts**, plus a couple of clever bill-splitting setups that let four flatmates pay less than two would on a normal contract.

Here's the actual cheapest options for September 2026 onwards.

## The student broadband landscape (May 2026)

Three things are true about the UK student broadband market right now:

1. **Standard 12-month contracts are still cheaper per month** than student-specific 9-month deals — but only if you actually use all 12 months.
2. **Student-specific contracts have crept up in price** since 2024 (BT student went from £25 to £29.99 in 18 months).
3. **Full-fibre coverage is now in 78% of UK student postcodes**, according to Ofcom's April 2026 report — so you don't have to settle for slow copper-based packages anymore.

## The cheapest 9-month student broadband contracts

These are the deals genuinely available in May 2026, sorted by total cost over the contract length.

### Vodafone Student Fibre 1 (38 Mbps) — £20/mo, 9 months

The cheapest legitimate student broadband on the market. Vodafone runs this as a "Student Pro" tier on a regular Fibre 1 connection. You get £180 total cost over 9 months, no setup fee, free router.

**Catch**: 38 Mbps is fine for one or two people but starts choking at 4-person households doing simultaneous Netflix + Zoom calls.

### Hyperoptic Student Fibre 50 (50 Mbps) — £24/mo, 9 months

Available only at specific apartment blocks Hyperoptic has wired (mostly purpose-built student housing). Worth checking your postcode at hyperoptic.com — if it's available, this is the best mid-range option.

### Sky Broadband Essential Student (36 Mbps) — £25/mo, 9 months

Sky's offer is more flexible: you can extend month-to-month after the initial 9 if you stay over summer, or just cancel. £225 total, free router, no setup.

### Virgin Media O2 Student Volt (132 Mbps) — £30/mo, 9 months

The fastest student-specific deal. £270 total cost, comes bundled with O2 mobile data boost (extra 5GB on any O2 SIM in the household). Catch: cable, not fibre — your speed depends on local cable health.

## When a regular 12-month contract is actually cheaper

This is the maths nobody does until it's too late.

If you're at uni for the **full academic year + staying for summer work**, a regular 12-month contract beats a student deal almost every time. Compare:

| Be Fibre 12-mo standard | £24.50 | **£294** |

## The bill-splitting setup that beats every student deal

Four flatmates, one Be Fibre 1000 Mbps gigabit contract, split four ways. Here's the maths.

**Be Fibre Gigafast 12-mo**: £39.50/mo, 1000 Mbps download/upload, free router, no setup fee.

Split four ways = **£9.88 per person per month**. That's cheaper than any individual student deal, and you each get 250 Mbps minimum.

**The catch**: someone has to be the bill-payer. They need to be staying for the full 12 months (the others can leave and you replace them). The sensible setup:

1. Pick the housemate who's a postgrad / masters / staying for summer
2. Use a shared payment app (Splitwise is free, Trickle is £1/mo for direct-debit splitting)
3. Add it to a written house agreement — small claims court is messy with broadband bills

This setup needs **all four people to commit for at least 9 months**. If one person bails after 3 months and isn't replaced, the bill-payer is on the hook for £20+ extra each month.

## What about no-contract / monthly rolling broadband?

Three options exist:

1. **NOW Broadband Brilliant Fibre** — £30/mo no contract, but charges £40 setup. Worth it only if you're staying 2-3 months.
2. **Vodafone PAYG Mobile broadband** — £35/mo unlimited 5G, no contract, just plug in a 5G hub. Speeds depend on Vodafone signal at your address (test before you commit).
3. **Three's 5G Home Broadband** — £24/mo, 1-month rolling, no setup. Only works in postcodes with good Three 5G coverage.

For 90% of students staying 9+ months, no-contract is more expensive than a 9-month deal. Only worth it if you're genuinely month-to-month.

## Avoiding the September-rush price hike

UK broadband providers raise their headline prices around mid-August, knowing students panic-sign in September. A deal that's £25 in July becomes £29.99 in September.

The hack: **if you know your address from June onwards, sign in July**. Most providers will defer the start date by 6-8 weeks for free if you ask. You lock in the lower price and don't pay for empty months.
