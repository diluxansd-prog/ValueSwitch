## Which providers to AVOID for EU travel

Three big networks make EU roaming genuinely expensive in 2026:

**EE (£2.59/day)** — fair use cap means heavy users get throttled after 50GB. Their "Roam Plus" upgrade is £15/month bolted on top.

**Vodafone (£2.81/day)** — the most expensive of the four major UK networks. Plans now require an "Xtra" upgrade to include roaming, which adds £4-£6/month to whatever you were paying.

**O2 (£2.59/day on newer plans)** — older O2 plans (2021 and earlier) still have free roaming, but anything signed since mid-2022 charges. Read the small print.

## What to check before signing any UK SIM contract for travel

1. **Country list** — "EU roaming" doesn't always mean every EU country. Lebara covers 27, Three covers 71 destinations, EE covers 47. Check the specific list before booking your trip.
2. **Fair use cap** — even "free" roaming usually caps data at 5-25GB before throttling. If you stream Netflix on holiday, you'll hit it.
3. **Tethering** — some plans block hotspot use abroad even if it's allowed at home. Check the small print.
4. **Voice/SMS** — most "free" deals only cover data; calls and texts back to UK numbers may still cost extra.
5. **Activation time** — eSIM travel SIMs activate instantly; physical SIMs take 2-3 days to ship. Plan ahead.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is EU roaming free for UK contracts in 2026?

No — Brexit ended the "Roam Like At Home" obligation in 2022. Most UK networks now charge between £1 and £2.81 per day for EU roaming. A handful (Lebara, giffgaff to a limit, Virgin Media) still include it free. Always check your specific contract's terms.

### Which UK SIM-only is cheapest with free EU roaming?

**Lebara at £4.50/month** for 5GB on a 12-month plan — free roaming included in 39 destinations. For unlimited data, **Lebara's £20/month 100GB plan** is the cheapest option with free EU coverage.

### Can I use my UK mobile in non-EU European countries (Switzerland, Norway, Turkey)?

Most UK networks treat non-EU European destinations as "rest of world" with much higher charges. Check each provider's destination list — Lebara covers Switzerland and Turkey free; Three's £2/day fair use covers Norway and Iceland. Vodafone and EE charge £6+/day for these.

### Do travel eSIMs work on iPhones?

Yes — every iPhone XS and newer supports eSIMs. Most Android flagships from 2021 onwards do too. Check your phone's compatibility before buying. Travel eSIMs from Sim Local, Airalo and Holafly install via QR code in 60 seconds.

### What's the cheapest way to use my UK number while abroad?

If you need to keep your UK number active (for SMS verification codes, banking apps), keep your UK SIM in slot 1 with mobile data turned OFF, and put a travel eSIM in slot 2 for data. You'll receive UK SMS for free, browse on the cheap travel data, and only pay for incoming calls (which are usually free anyway).

### How do I avoid getting hit with surprise roaming charges?

Three rules: (1) before you fly, log into your network's app and turn ON the roaming caps if available — they prevent you spending more than £40 a month abroad without your consent; (2) turn off automatic app updates and iCloud Photo backup before you board; (3) connect to hotel Wi-Fi for video calls and large downloads.
